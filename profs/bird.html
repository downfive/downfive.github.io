Like the rest of her family, Birdstar is incredibly large compared to the average cat, standing about a half-foot or so taller than most. While her fur isn't particularly long, its very thick and dense, keeping her well-insulated in the colder months and making her utterly miserable when it gets warmer. Underneath her heavy coat lie large, powerful muscles, furthering her imposing appearance.

Her patterning is nothing too notable. The majority of her body is white, pulled from her father. The top of her head, her back, and her tail are caped by a silvery-gray color. Darker gray tabby stripes run throughout, and are most prominent along her tail. Her eyes are a pale, yellow-shaded green.

As a kit and an apprentice, Birdstar was loud, snappy, bullheaded, and a staunch advocate for the justification of murder. But being thrust into a position of power young, made deputy very recently after her warrior ceremony, forced her to shake off some of the amoral fire of youth and trade it for civility.

Her leader ceremony furthered this growth. Though she was forced under the mantle (barely a season after her deputy ceremony) following her predecessors disappearance during the earthquake, the lives granted to her there were ones of love, friendship, of joy and care for the world. Though she cared little for them prior to that, and is largely neutral now, Birdstar tries to take the advice given to her there to heart as well as she can.

Birdstar is generally good-natured and easy to find humor with, despite her resting expression making her seem less than approachable. She cares deeply for her Clanmates and for her family, something she considers foundational to her position as leader. There's a bit of sentimental philosopher in her too, when the moment calls for it, but she tends to say little more than necessary otherwise.
